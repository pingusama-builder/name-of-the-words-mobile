# Name of the Words: App Introduction

## Philosophy

**to name is to evoke**

Name of the Words is a thoughtfully designed platform for collecting, rating, and exploring words that resonate across languages, contexts, and meanings. It recognizes that words are not mere labels—they are vessels of understanding, carriers of cultural weight, and bridges between concepts. Each word becomes a portal to deeper comprehension of language, thought, and self.

---

## Core Concept

In a world of infinite information, words matter. But not all words matter equally. Some words capture something ineffable—a feeling, a concept, a truth that ordinary language struggles to express. Some words are beautiful. Some are essential. Some are subtle, revealing themselves only through prolonged contemplation.

Name of the Words invites you to curate your personal lexicon. To mark the words that move you. To understand them across multiple dimensions. To connect them across languages and contexts. To build a living collection that grows with your understanding.

---

## Unique Features

### 1. **Multidimensional Rating System**

Words are not one-dimensional. A word can be:

- **Essence** (0-12): How fundamentally important or true does this word feel? Does it capture something real about the world?
- **Beauty** (0-12): How aesthetically or emotionally resonant is this word? Does it please you when spoken or read?
- **Subtlety** (0-12): How much depth does this word contain? How many layers of meaning does it reveal over time?

Each word receives a **Match Percentage** calculated from these three dimensions. A perfect match (100%) represents a word that is essential, beautiful, and subtle all at once—rare and precious.

**Design insight:** Rather than a single star rating, this system honors the complexity of language. A word can be profoundly essential but aesthetically plain (like "water"). Another can be beautiful but less subtle (like "serendipity"). The three-axis approach captures this nuance.

### 2. **Paired Meanings Across Languages**

Language is not translation—it is transformation. A Chinese word cannot be "translated" into English; it can only be paired with an English word that captures a related but distinct concept.

Name of the Words allows you to:

- Collect a word in any language
- Pair it with a word from another language that resonates with similar meaning
- Rate the match percentage between them (how well do they align?)
- Add context explaining the connection

This feature reveals the gaps and bridges between languages. It shows how different cultures conceptualize the same reality differently. It becomes a tool for linguistic and cultural exploration.

**Design insight:** By focusing on "pairing" rather than "translation," the app acknowledges that languages are not interchangeable. It celebrates difference rather than erasing it.

### 3. **Dual Collection Modes**

#### Normal Mode
Collect freely. Add words as you encounter them. Build your personal lexicon without constraints. This mode is for discovery, for following your curiosity, for building a collection that reflects your interests and encounters.

#### Work Mode
Focus deeply on a curated subset. Use this mode when you're researching a specific topic, preparing for a project, or diving deep into a particular domain. Work mode words are visually distinct (marked with a "Work" badge) and can be toggled on/off to filter your view.

**Design insight:** Most word collection apps offer a single mode. By providing two, Name of the Words acknowledges that users collect for different reasons. Sometimes you want breadth; sometimes you want depth. The app adapts to your rhythm.

### 4. **Mutual-Arising Mode**

Some words belong to both Normal and Work collections. They are words you encounter casually but also study deeply. Words that bridge your casual interests and focused work.

The **Mutual-Arising** feature allows you to:

- Mark any word as "Mutual" when adding it
- Toggle a global "Mutual-Arising Mode" to see words from both collections merged
- Migrate existing words between modes (Normal ↔ Work ↔ Mutual-Arising)
- Search across all modes simultaneously

Words marked as "Mutual-Arising" are visible in all modes and searchable everywhere, creating true interconnection between your collections.

**Design insight:** Rather than forcing users into a single organizational system, Mutual-Arising acknowledges that real-world knowledge doesn't fit neatly into boxes. It allows organic, fluid organization that reflects how people actually think.

### 5. **Rich Contextual Fields**

Each word entry includes:

- **Meaning**: The definition or interpretation in your own words
- **Context**: A sentence or passage where you encountered this word, or how you use it
- **Source**: Where you found this word (book, conversation, article, etc.)
- **Location**: Geographic or cultural origin (optional)
- **Tags**: Flexible categorization (e.g., "philosophy," "emotion," "nature")

**Design insight:** By providing multiple contextual fields, the app recognizes that words are not isolated units. They live in sentences, books, conversations, cultures. Context is as important as the word itself.

### 6. **Full-Text Search**

Search across all fields—not just the word itself. Find words by:

- Their meaning
- The context they appear in
- Their source
- Their tags
- Their location

This enables discovery. You might search "Shakespeare" to find all words from Shakespeare's works. Or search "emotion" to find all words you've tagged as emotional. Or search "Mandarin" to find all words from Chinese sources.

**Design insight:** Most apps search only the word name. Full-text search treats the entire entry as a searchable document, enabling serendipitous discovery and pattern recognition.

### 7. **Always-Accessible Add Button**

The + button lives in the top-right header, always visible and accessible. No scrolling required. No hidden menus. Adding a word is a first-class action, not an afterthought.

**Design insight:** The placement and prominence of the add button reflects the app's core purpose: collecting words. It's not buried in a menu or relegated to a floating button that obscures content. It's part of the interface itself.

## Design Philosophy

### Minimalism with Depth

The interface is clean and minimal—no unnecessary decoration, no visual clutter. But beneath the surface lies profound depth: multidimensional ratings, contextual fields, cross-language pairing, mode switching. The app respects your intelligence and invites exploration.

### Intentional Typography

The app uses serif fonts (Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond) for titles and quotes. Serif fonts evoke tradition, literature, and thoughtfulness. They signal that this is a space for contemplation, not quick consumption.

### Dark Theme with Teal Accents

The dark background reduces eye strain and creates an intimate, focused environment. The teal accent color (#4f98a3) suggests calm, clarity, and intellectual depth—colors associated with contemplation and understanding.

### Smooth Animations

Transitions are smooth and purposeful. Buttons fade in. Cards slide. The interface feels alive but not frenetic. Animations serve function, not decoration.

### Responsive Design

The app works seamlessly on mobile, tablet, and desktop. The layout adapts, but the core experience remains consistent. Whether you're collecting words on your phone during a commute or reviewing your collection on a desktop, the experience is thoughtful and intentional.

## Use Cases

### For Language Learners
Collect words in your target language and pair them with words in your native language. Understand not just the translation, but the conceptual differences between languages.

### For Writers
Build a personal thesaurus of words that resonate with you. Rate them across multiple dimensions. Understand the subtle differences between similar words. Use the search to find the perfect word for your work.

### For Researchers
Organize words by source, location, and context. Use Work Mode to focus on a specific research project. Use tags to categorize findings. Use search to discover patterns across your research.

### For Curious Minds
Simply collect words that move you. Build a personal lexicon that reflects your interests, encounters, and growth over time. Revisit your collection to see how your understanding has evolved.

## Key Metrics

The app tracks:

- **Seekers**: Total number of users who have engaged with the platform
- **Words Named**: Total number of words collected across all users

These metrics celebrate the collective effort of the community—thousands of people, each curating their own lexicon, together building a shared appreciation for language.

## The Name

**Name of the Words** is inspired by Patrick Rothfuss's *The Name of the Wind*, which explores the power of true names and the magic of language. The Chinese subtitle **言之名** (yán zhī míng) translates roughly to "the name of words" or "the naming of language"—a poetic reflection of the app's purpose.

The tagline **"to name is to evoke"** captures the core belief: naming something is not a neutral act. It is an act of creation, of understanding, of bringing something into being through language.

## The Experience

When you open Name of the Words, you encounter:

1. A clean, minimal interface that invites focus
2. A tagline that sets the philosophical tone
3. A rotating quote about the power of names and language
4. Global statistics showing the collective effort of the community
5. Two simple buttons: + (to add a word) and > (to enter your collection)

Once inside, you find:

- Your collection organized by mode (Normal, Work, or both)
- The ability to search across all fields
- The ability to rate words across three dimensions
- The ability to pair words across languages
- The ability to migrate words between modes
- The ability to tag, contextualize, and source every word

The experience is intentional. Every element serves a purpose. Every interaction feels considered.

---

## The Philosophy Behind the Design

Name of the Words rejects several common assumptions:

1. **Not a productivity tool.** It's not about checking boxes or maximizing output. It's about depth, contemplation, and understanding.

2. **Not a social network.** There's no comparison, no competition, no pressure to collect more than others. Your collection is personal and private.

3. **Not a quick reference.** This is not a dictionary app. It's a space for slow, deliberate engagement with language.

4. **Not a translation tool.** It acknowledges that languages are different, not interchangeable. It celebrates these differences rather than erasing them.

5. **Not a one-size-fits-all solution.** It offers multiple modes, multiple rating dimensions, multiple organizational systems—because people collect words for different reasons.

---

## Conclusion

Name of the Words is an invitation to slow down. To pay attention to language. To understand that words matter—not just as communication tools, but as vessels of meaning, carriers of culture, and bridges between understanding.

It is a space for the curious, the contemplative, the linguistically minded. A space where collecting words is not a hobby, but a practice. A practice of attention, of understanding, of connection.

**To name is to evoke.** And in evoking, we understand.
